Terra Firma

 

Poem
May 30, 2019

I love ugly ducklings.
They do flutter in the plague tempest,
smiling inside like sailors with oranges.
Nothing means that they have the world
between their frocks.
Time tells you what to expect.
Those babes sure do love that green gogo,
don't matter what time or day.
Throw it out the window is what I say.
Who do you think made up the month of May?
Do you really believe the story of the way
he came to gray?
The wind of the hottest desert tempest
could not break his sweat.
He threw his seed out of the window so it would
grow wings.
Fly on the plague storms,
he did.
